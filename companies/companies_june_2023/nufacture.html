Initial Thoughts

  • Curious to understand the validity of the painpoint nufacture is solving - is searching for these components truly difficult/scattered? What are the current search mechanisms in place? If the problem is valid, and existing infastructure for this search doesn't exist, then I think the problem would be widespread and thus exploitable for a growing business
  • Interested in the audience he is targeting, benefits of certain business models (B2B, D2C, enterprise subscription)
  • Curious to see if there already exists some type of search mechanism in any other software that the industrial-sector uses that either contains a similar search-for-parts mechanism or could include this mechanism
  • Freeman has extensive experience within the industrial sector (both installation and management), sets him up well to understand the needs of the industry and possibly the network to connect with potential customers; well positioned to be the person who solves it

Verticles and Customers

Existing business model is unclear from website but thoughts on business model below...
I think there are two sides of the platform that Nufacture could extract revenue from:

  • Revenue from buyer: whether this is a subscription to use the service or a fee charged on a per purchase basis
  • Revenue from seller: the site has a distribution network to showcase parts, prices, and avaliability from select retailers - why not charge a service fee to host your catologue online or have an affiliate percentage from purchases that go through the site?
Creating this two sided marketplace allows you to extract revenue from two parties.
