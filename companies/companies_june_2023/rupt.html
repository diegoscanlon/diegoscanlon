Initial Thoughts

  • Netflix crackdown on password sharing to increase individual subscriptions seems like a solid proof of concept that decreasing account sharing is a valid approach to increasing revenue
  • Problem seems widespread in many domains that use family subscription models for discounted rates that users take advantage of
  • Mechanism to implement Rupt seems straightforward as you're plugging into your own system, founder is experienced technical lead and a repeat founder
  • Could benefit from an incubator through network growth, especially in the United States, and showcase the technology to more companies with these subscription models to grow untapped revenue

Product Features

Leaving money on the table by cracking down on account sharing
Primary features include:
  • Shared account detection
  • Repeat trial detection (know and limit free-trial abuse by repeat users)
  • Advanced signal detection (unique and advanced signals of illegal account sharing)
  • Ghost-user estimation (shared accounts have more than one person, Rupt tells you how many)
Other features include:
  • UI to nudge users to subscribe (ready made blocking dialogue to nudge account sharers to subscribe)
  • Suspend repeat abusers
  • Whitelist devices

Thoughts and Conclusion

Netflix recently cracked down on password sharing
  • Either kick people off their Netflix account or pay subscription for an additional membership for those outside their main household
  • Process included "a way for current subscribers to view which devices are signed into their account and remove those that shouldn't have access"
  • Transfer Profile: helps relocated users transfer existing account information, including viewing history and watchlist
Would be curious to see if Netflix crackdown technology was created internally or externally, would give some insight into the cost associated with a company building this out on their own
  • Clearly other subscription video entertainment companies like Netflix (HBO Max, Paramount, Disney) that could probably benefit from the reactionary research that Netflix has done
  • Other subscription based companies (Spotify) that offer family plans (under one roof) are being exploited and could probably use this tracking of multiple accounts.

Mechanism for the technology is simple: plug in Rupt SDKs into your project, takes less than 1 day
Founder should be focused on getting larger companies to test the product - hurdle may be convincing companies that users will actually sign up instead of just abandoning the subscription all together; seems to be dependent on conversion when nudged
